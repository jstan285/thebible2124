• Use of imperatives emphasizes empowerment rather than mere command.
• Asymmetric construction — sea creatures addressed directly; birds described in third person.

Poetic/Chiastic Patterns:
• Thematic triad: be fruitful / multiply / fill — rhythmic momentum toward abundance.
• Structural balance between water and land life.

Reused Narrative Forms:
• Blessing speech-form recurs later in v.28 with humans — parallel structures.

[SYMBOLISM AND POTENTIAL INTERPRETATIONS]

Blessing introduces a deeper relational mode — not just function, but favor.

“Fruitfulness” as sacred impulse — creation isn’t just populated, it is endowed to expand.

Sea and sky as domains that now pulse with blessing — life is not static, it is catalytic.

Birds multiplying “in the land” = boundary-crossing — aerial beings tethered to terrestrial rhythms.
