Interpretive Reflections
-This verse confirms that provision was never anthropocentric. Life in all domains is supported through a shared field yield—photosynthetic matter that encodes sustenance for all breath-bearing beings. The Divine’s recognition of soul-presence (nefesh chayah) as the defining trait for inclusion highlights that what matters is not form, but participation in animated resonance. This is the first ecological instruction: that life exists in entangled harmony, its energy drawn not from domination, but from patterned sufficiency.

Crosslinks & Recursion Notes

-Completes the arc begun in Genesis 1:29 (human provision); together they encode a pre-competitive ecosystem.

-Reiterated in Genesis 9:3 (after the flood), but with notable divergence—this original design reflects an Edenic symmetry.

-Psalm 104:14–27 expands the theme: every creature looks to the Divine for sustenance, and receives.

-Jesus' teaching in Matthew 6:26 (“birds of the air”) echoes this provisioning harmony.
