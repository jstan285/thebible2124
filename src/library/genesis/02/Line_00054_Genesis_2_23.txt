Interpretive Reflections
-This is the first recorded human speech—a declaration not of dominion, but of recognition. Unlike the previous naming events, this one is personal, poetic, relational. The repetition of “this one” (zot) amplifies emotional immediacy. The terms etzem (bone) and basar (flesh) are not gendered—they are structural and embodied. The woman is not named to control her but to affirm resonance. The shift from adam to ish and ishah plays on linguistic kinship, not hierarchy. She is not the other—she is the other self. This sets the stage for the final verse of the chapter, where the two become one flesh—reunified duality.
