[STRUCTURAL PATTERN NOTES]

Syntactic Observations:
• Begins with a clear subject-verb-object structure — unusual amid the poetic-legal speech blocks.
• The “for she was…” clause clarifies motivation, reflecting interpretive theology in narrative syntax.

Poetic/Chiastic Patterns:
• Echoes earlier structure (2:23) where the man names woman in response to union.
• Now names her “Chavvah” in response to disunion, mortality, and generativity.

Reused Narrative Forms:
• Naming formula appears across Genesis as a mark of agency (e.g., 4:17, 5:3).
• “Chavvah” here anticipates the life-bringing lineages of Gen 4–5.

[SYMBOLISM AND POTENTIAL INTERPRETATIONS]

“Chavvah” = life; a profound gesture in the immediate shadow of death (3:19).

Eve becomes the living bridge across the rupture — a symbolic seed of resilience.

Naming as act of recognition, not possession: she is honored for her role.
