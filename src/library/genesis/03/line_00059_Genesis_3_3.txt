Interpretive Reflections
-This is the first recorded addition to Divine speech by a human agent. The Divine never prohibited touching the tree—only eating. The woman’s addition may stem from protective intention, but it now reframes the Divine as more restrictive than reality. The phrase “lest you die” is accurate in tone but lacks the weight of the original Hebrew warning from Genesis 2:17 (“you shall surely die”)—a certainty is now softened to a possibility. The field is shifting not from rebellion, but from uncertain memory and defensive interpretation. This is the subtle beginning of observer drift.
