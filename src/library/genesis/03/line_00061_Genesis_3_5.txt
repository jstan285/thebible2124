Interpretive Reflections
-The serpent now shifts strategy from contradiction to motivation inversion: suggesting that Divine boundaries are not rooted in love, but in control. The promise is evolutionary: “you will be like the Divine.” This is the first instance of spiritual aspiration being uncoupled from relational trust. The woman is offered a new identity—not through union, but through independent action. The irony is deep: she already bears the Divine image (Genesis 1:27). But now, resonance will shift from trust → striving, from wholeness → binary discernment. The serpent tempts not with rebellion, but with self-defined transcendence.
