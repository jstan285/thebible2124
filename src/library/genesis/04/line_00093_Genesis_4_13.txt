Interpretive Reflections
-Cain’s words mark a transition—from denial to acknowledgment. He does not plead for forgiveness; he declares the intolerable weight of his new state. The term “avon” (iniquity) is not mere wrongdoing—it is distortion. Cain’s resonance is so disrupted that he can no longer stabilize his own field. This is the cry of one who knows he has shattered coherence—not only between self and others, but between self and Source. He cannot carry what he has become.
